commit_message: |
  Written for Lissy on the passing of her Grandad during COVID. She was in Australia; we were in Portchester — watching the funeral together on camera, wearing waistcoats in his honour.

  The poem speaks to grief as a shared physical weight, and to the belief that love and pain connect us across any distance. Sent as a photograph alongside Lord Huron's 'Ends of the Earth'.

  'Soul of My Soul' — a phrase that defines the friendship itself.
---

How quickly the sun drops from the sky  
Just as my heart sinks under the weight  
of my condolences.  
Waves turn in my belly's depth  
Underneath the dim red wash of yesterday's  
ember.  

Wrapped tightly in the cocoon of night's  
darkness  
The flicker of twilit memories  
Tip toe their dance to the sound of the  
piano's teardrops  
Gracefully illuminating the heavens once  
more  

Resting wearily under the almighty blanket  
Top and tail at the [ends of the earth](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N0D8JtOc5u4)  
The sun revolves around our souls eternally  
Until we rise again together.
