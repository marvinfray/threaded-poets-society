I hear the world singing,
Not in unison but in pieces, fractured refrains—
Each voice a part, claiming space in the cacophony.

Here, a melody struggles to rise above the din,
There, a harmony stumbles, unsure of its place.

The smith still pounds steel in his forge,
But now his song echoes through machines,
His hammering lost beneath the hum of automation.

The farmer's hymn is swallowed
By sprawling cities that rise like tidal waves.

The tailor hums softly, but her thread
Unravels, pulled taut by unseen hands.

And we, the singers of the global world,
Sing beneath a light so bright it burns.
Eyes everywhere; the gaze is relentless,
The spotlight casting shadows on our own reflections.

We see each other, we see ourselves,
But in the glare, we imagine walls where there are none,
Boundaries drawn where unity might dwell.

Each part believes itself alone,
A soloist drowning in its own sound.
The crisis is a mirror, reflecting separation—
Yet within the cracks lies the chorus unspoken.

So hard, as the underground laments,
We just want to be free,
To shed the weight of the relentless light,
To breathe in a night where all is shadowed,
Where the imagined lines dissolve.

*De noche, todos los gatos son pardos—*
In the darkness, all cats are gray.
And so we gather, as fathers foresaw,
Around the fires, beneath the stars,
In the sugar shacks and hidden spaces,
We sing — not apart but together.

Listen: the rhythm of feet crossing borders,
The whisper of hands extended in peace,
The ancient pulse of the oceans that bind us,
A song older than the maps we draw.
The silence between the notes, holding space for the whole.

I hear the world imagining—
Tentative, yes, but daring to sing together.
The song not yet written, the harmony not yet found,
But the beat is there, beneath it all,
Softening the fracture, drawing the parts close.
We are not separate — never were.
The song is ours, and it has always been.
