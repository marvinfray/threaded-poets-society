commit_message: |
  A poem from a Sunday spinning records with a friend.
  It cycles the way the set did: low to high to low, earth to air to
  earth, gripping and reaching and coming back to ground. The Rick and
  Morty quotes carry the swing — anxiety as false height, "don't think
  about it" turning from deflection into release. Underneath it all,
  the wordless conversation two people can have through record selection.
---

Low end first.  
The kick you feel before you hear it,  
a borrowed heartbeat in the floorboards —  
we start the way all things start,  
in the ground, in the gut,  
roots reading the dark by touch.  

You pull a record. I pull a record.  
Neither of us says what we mean.  
The music says it for us.  

Then the hi-hats come, like weather,  
and the riser does what risers do —  
winds the spring, thins the air,  
takes us up past where the bass can reach,  
up where the head goes spinning  
on its little stem of neck —  

*I'm starting to work up some anxiety* —  

and there it is, the false height,  
the reach that forgot its roots,  
all canopy and no soil,  
thinking, thinking, thinking —  

*don't think about it.*  

Drop.  

And the body takes the off-ramp gratefully.  
The chest remembers what the skull forgot:  
that the low end was holding us the whole time,  
that the ground was never gone,  
only out-climbed.  

You pull a record. I pull a record.  
We are saying things  
we'd struggle to say at a kitchen table.  

Again the build. Again the climb.  
Because the tree is not embarrassed to reach —  
the reaching feeds the roots,  
the leaves send sugar down the dark,  
and what grows tall on purpose  
is not the same as what spins loose.  

*I'm starting to work up some anxiety* —  
yes, friend, that's just the riser talking.  

*Don't think about it* —  
no, not denial this time. Release.  
The thought set down like a needle lifted,  
the groove still turning underneath.  

By the third pass it is almost prayer:  
low, high, low —  
earth, air, earth —  
grip, reach, return —  

and the last record plays itself out  
to two men nodding in a Sunday room,  
rooted, lifted,  
not thinking about it  
at all.
