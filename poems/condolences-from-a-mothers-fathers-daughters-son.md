---
title: "Condolences from A Mother's Father's, Daughter's Son"
date: 2024-12-17
tags: [grief, family, sea, loss]
commit_message: |
  A poem written for a friend on learning of her father's passing — she who, years earlier, had stood beside me in my own grief after losing my mother, and given me the language and practices to carry it.

  The poem is set at Cwm yr Eglwys, Wales — where our family retreated after my mother died. I remember a bench, a stormy sea, a stranger remarking she's angry today. I felt that completely. The next morning the water was still. Behind me throughout, the collapsed church — worn down by time and weather, bearing witness without offering shelter.

  She held me like a mother when I needed it most. This was written the moment I learned she was now a daughter in grief. The sea that once carried my rage became the same sea carrying her father's memory. Salt touching both wounds alike.

  The hollow church appears here before it appears anywhere else in the work — a ruin that watches rather than protects. It would return.

  A poem of return. What she gave me, offered back.
footnote: "grief passes between generations like salt water; the sea that roared back one person's loss becomes the cradle of another's — the same shore, different mourning"
---

She, who held me like a mother,
stands now, a daughter in grief.
The sea carries her father's memory,
as it once cradled my mother's absence,
salt touching both wounds alike.

It once cradled my mother's absence,
stormy, fierce — roaring back my rage.
Before me, the sea opened wide,
between the cliffs' dark, gaping mouth,
and behind me, the ruins watched —
a graveyard's stones, the hollow church,
bearing witness to my grief.
