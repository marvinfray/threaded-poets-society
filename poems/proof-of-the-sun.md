The weekend closes  
like an album I'm not ready to put down.  

I keep reaching —  
not for yesterday exactly,  
more for the particular warmth  
that holds us the way the sun holds everything —  
not diminished by distance,  
not troubled by cloud,  
transcendent of all of it,  
quietly keeping us both  
whether we feel it or not.  

And lately we feel it in slivers.  
Spring still remembering how much of itself to give,  
finding us in pockets of garden,  
gaps in the grey —  
before the machine called us back.  
Barely. Begrudgingly.  
The inbox. The calendar. The wheels  
of a working week built for production,  
never for standing in a garden  
when the light comes through.  

But here is what the clouds teach —  
absence doesn't diminish the sun.  
It reveals it.  
Strips away the taken-for-granted,  
leaves the heart suddenly awake  
to what was always that large,  
always that constant,  
always that close.  

So I thumb back through pictures.  
Looking for your face.  
And finding it —  
the proof of the sun,  
each photograph a gap in the cloud  
where the light found us  
and I remembered  
what I already knew.
