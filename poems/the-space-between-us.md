  On what gets crushed when AI collapses every gap — silence, intention, spirit.
  The poem began as a reflection on a conversation about AI architecture: the vision of seamless, frictionless, pixel-to-pixel intelligence with no API between interactions. No door to knock on. No threshold.
  The more I sat with it, the more it felt like suffocation. The kind that happens when you optimise away the pause before someone speaks, the moment of not-saying that means everything.
---

They want to close the gap —  
every silence a latency,  
every pause a bug to fix,  
the in-between a nothing  
they would rather not pay for.  

But the gap is where I put the question.  
The gap is where you feel the weight of it.  

Feed it pixels, return it pixels,  
no door to knock on,  
no threshold where a word might rest  
before it enters —  
just continuous, seamless,  
frictionless,  
the dream of engineers  
who've never watched a friend  
not say something  
and mean everything.  

Between the asking and the answering  
something lives —  
intention, doubt, the flicker  
of a feeling half-formed,  
philosophy not yet hardened into position,  
spirit moving  
on the face of the water.  

Crush it out.  
Press the life from it.  
Seal every gap until nothing breathes —  
not meaning, not mystery,  
not the unnamed thing  
that moves between two people  
when words have run out  
and silence becomes the sentence.  

Optimise the holy.  
Reduce the nave to corridor,  
the corridor to wire.  

We built cathedrals with high ceilings  
not for God  
but for the echo  
of our own voices  
asking if anyone is there.
