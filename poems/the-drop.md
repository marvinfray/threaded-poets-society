<pre>
I arrive at the fire knowing what fire is.
                                              I know this.
Convection. Exothermic. The chemistry
                                         of absence —
                   what wood becomes
                                        when it lets go.

We spoke of boys who've never been
                                        this close to a flame,
girls who've never heard a river
                                   at three in the morning —

                   and I was still thinking about it.
                                                          Still thinking.


            The water didn't wait for permission.

                        It entered through the sternum —
                                    not idea
                                                   but actual cold,
                              not language
                                                but the body's
                                                               yes.

                                    Everything I knew
                                                         dissolved
                                                                      downstream.

                                                      I stopped
                                                                naming.
                                                                           Stayed.
</pre>
