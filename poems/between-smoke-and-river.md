footnote: |
  Written after a stag ceremony — all of us around a fire, a river running through camp. The poem tries to enact what we discussed: water as the most intelligent substance in the universe, fire as the thing we learned to command.  
  The horizontal axis runs water (left) to fire (right).  
  The vertical drops from mind (air) to body (earth).  
  Top-right is where I spend most of my time.  
  Bottom-left is where I was always already from.  
commit_message: |
  This poem came from a weekend that asked me to stop thinking and remember I was made of something older. 
  We sat with fire but we were of the water — finding the lowest place, patient, more intelligent than anything we've named. 
  The structure tries to be honest about that: my mind arrives top-right, wanting credit. 
  The body has been waiting, bottom-left, since before the word for low. The single word 'ash' at the end is what fire leaves when it's done commanding.

<pre>
the river doesn't need to be told.
                I —             named the fire first
it has been finding the lowest place
                                the smoke rising from it
since before the word for low.
                took hold of it
                called it ours


      we sat with it anyway, the five of us,
      around it,
      passing it hand to hand
      in the old way 

                                the smoke lifting through us
the river keeping low


the body knows this register.
                                the mind arrived late
                cold entering the sternum
                wanting credit
                                like a question
that doesn't need an answer
                
        reaching upward
        trying to hold it

        somewhere between the smoke and the current
        a boy I used to be —
                (again) —
                stopped performing warmth


        and let the water
        think for him.
                (she was better at it.)

ash.
</pre>
