---
title: The Same Moon
date: 2026-03-05
tags: [tide, distance, time]
commit_message: |
  This poem emerged from personal reflection on fatherhood, friction, and the desire to be more present.

  The emotional root was a moment of noticing: that when discomfort rises, the instinct is either to rush through it or resist it, and that both impulses come from the same place. That awareness became the foundation.

  From there the reflection expanded to acknowledge a truth that no parent meets every need, and no child receives a complete version of their parent. Love is distributed unevenly across time. That is not failure — it is timing.

  Then came another metaphor — from wondering about how much a vein in a leaf or the path of a river follows rules or patterns, versus the unique beauty born of taking its own course. Tides hold connection and distance, exposure and return, cosmic influence and human powerlessness, pattern and variation — all at once.

  We are shaped by the same forces, but never in the same way.
footnote: "the tide does not bargain; the shore must learn the rhythm it is given — what is uncovered is not failure, only what was always there, waiting for the right light"
---

At low tide the shore reveals its thinking —
lines in the sand repeating themselves
with a patience that feels older than water.
Each curve familiar,
each detail unrepeatable,
as if the sea remembers the pattern
but not the moment.

In one place, spring light lengthens the day.
A shoreline quick with change,
footprints crossing and recrossing
before they can settle.
The tide comes in with energy there,
arrives early,
leaves questions half held in its wake.

Elsewhere, the same water arrives differently.
First touch on unmarked sand,
a brightness not yet shaped by return.
Though the air begins to cool,
something of warmth lingers in the name,
as if the season has not quite agreed
to let itself go.

Not far along the coast, though not the same coast,
another edge has known a longer pull.
The sea has come and gone there many times,
cut deeper in places,
softened others without asking.
No two tides have spoken to it the same way,
yet each has left something behind.

We stand sometimes wanting the water to stay —
to fill every hollow,
to meet every edge at once.
But the moon does not bargain with us.
It draws what it draws,
and the shore must learn
the rhythm it is given.

What is uncovered is not failure.
It is simply what was always there —
lines waiting for light,
questions waiting for time.
Distance is not absence,
only the turning of things
beyond our keeping.

And still the tide returns.
Not as it was,
but carrying the same quiet intention.
The pattern holds,
even as it changes,
and we, like the shore,
are shaped by what comes and goes.
