*She's angry today.*
Three words, offered to no one in particular,
the way a man speaks to the sea
when he has known it long enough
to read its moods.
He had come the way he always came —
coat, hands, the habit of this place.
He did not know what he was walking toward.

Neither did I.
I had arrived by a longer road,
carried there by something
that had no language yet,
only weight.
The church behind us, already a ruin.
The cliffs holding the water
in their dark, open mouth.
The kind of place where land
admits it cannot go further.

He spoke. I received.
Between those two moments
something passed
that neither of us could account for —
nature and grief and the precise word
arriving at the same instant,
like a door opening
that had no handle on my side.
He did not know he held a key.
He did not know there was a lock.

No other words were spoken.
He moved on the way messengers do —
without ceremony,
without knowing.
And I sat with what remained:
the sea, still raging.
Myself, finally named.

The next morning she was still.
I do not know if I made her so,
or if she had simply
finished saying what needed saying.
Only that the water had changed,
and I had changed with it,
and behind me the hollow church
bore witness, as it always had,
to what passes through us
when we are finally ready to receive it.
