  Inspired by meditation on the pendulum of experience, and a reckoning within — something that often comes up for me in breath work.
  Today’s realisation: the pendulum isn’t a metaphor for binary thinking, but for the structure of a life in motion.

  The poem explores the swing between past and future, the still point of awareness,
  and how naming concepts and emotions creates space to meet them,
  rather than become them, or be swallowed by them.
---

the pendulum swings  
as it always has  
left into memory,  
right into imagining  

and I,  
somewhere in the arc—  
mistook the motion for myself  

until  
for a moment  
I stayed  

not stopped—  
but centred  

where nothing asks to be called anything  

---

once  
I had no words for the weather inside me  

storms came  
and I became them  

anger spoke in thunder  
grief in undertow  
fear in a tightening I could not translate  

everything unnamed  
everything everywhere  

---

then language arrived  
like small lanterns  

fear  
grief  
anger  

and suddenly,  
they stood at the edge of the firelight  

no longer the storm  
but visitors  

---

"what do you want?" I asked fear  

and it did not answer in threat  
but in memory  

"what are you holding?" I asked grief  

and it did not consume me  
but opened its hands  

---

to name a thing  
is not to trap it  

nor to banish it  

but to meet it  
just far enough away  
to see its face  

---

and still,  

there is a place  
before the naming  

before fear is fear  
before grief is grief  

where everything moves  
and nothing divides  

---

the pendulum passes through it  
every time  

but does not stay  

---

I think  
that place  

is not asking to be named  

only remembered
